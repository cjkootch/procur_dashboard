## 1. GL 48 framework — operational summary

This section restates the regulatory framework from the engagement brief §2 in operational language for use during contractor conversations, broker conversations, and counsel briefings.

### Authority

**OFAC General License 48** (issued 2026-02-10) authorizes transactions ordinarily incident and necessary to provision of goods, technology, software, or services for the **exploration, development, or production of oil or gas in Venezuela**, including transactions involving PDVSA Entities. **GL 48 is the operative authority for oilfield waste services as a service-provision activity in support of oil/gas production.**

### Compliance conditions specific to GL 48

For US-entity contractors directly engaging:
- Contracts with the Government of Venezuela or PDVSA Entities must specify **US law as governing**.
- Dispute resolution must occur **in the United States**.
- Any payments to blocked persons (excluding local taxes, permits, fees) must route to the **Foreign Government Deposit Funds (FGDF)** established under EO 14373 of 2026-01-09, or other Treasury-instructed accounts.
- **Reporting requirements** to State and Energy Departments within specified timelines after first transactions.

For non-US-entity contractors (including Vector Antilles):
- **FAQ 1247** confirms non-US persons engaging in transactions authorized by GL 48 do not face US secondary sanctions, *provided* they comply with GL conditions.
- Non-US persons must be organized under the laws of a third country **on or before 2025-01-29** to qualify for safe-harbor treatment.
- Vector Antilles eligibility under this temporal threshold: **counsel verification required**. Brief assumes Vector Antilles was organized in UAE before 2025-01-29; document the exact organizational date for counsel.

### Adjacent GLs that may bear on the engagement

- **GL 46A** — purchase / export / sale / refining of already-extracted Venezuelan-origin oil by established US entities. Relevant if recovered hydrocarbons from waste processing are sold to US entities.
- **GL 47** (2026-02-03) — transactions for export of US-origin diluents to Venezuela, including transactions involving PDVSA. Relevant if the contractor needs diluents in the recovery / processing chain.
- **GL 50A** — dispute resolution + contract enforcement transactions.
- **GL 51A / GL 52 / GL 54** — Venezuelan-origin minerals trading. Relevant if the recovery includes minerals/rare earths beyond hydrocarbons.

### Vector Antilles' compliance perimeter

Vector Antilles' role per the engagement brief is **brokerage + offtake intermediation, not direct PDVSA / mixta engagement**. The compliance perimeter:

- ✅ Identification + qualification of contractor candidates (this document)
- ✅ Engagement letter framework + commercial structuring with the contractor
- ✅ Offtake position on recovered hydrocarbons (FOB at processing site or after upgrading)
- ✅ Coordination with sanctions counsel + the fuel broker
- ❌ Direct engagement with mixtas (handled through contractor)
- ❌ Direct engagement with PDVSA (handled by qualified parties, not Vector Antilles)
- ❌ Operational execution of the waste processing work
- ❌ Capital commitment to the contractor or the work
- ❌ Any activity prior to counsel clearance

## 5. Hydrocarbon recovery offtake position

Per engagement brief §6 — the offtake position is the strongest demonstrable value Vector Antilles brings beyond contractor identification.

Three structures:

| Structure | Description | When |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Direct offtake** | Vector Antilles purchases recovered hydrocarbons FOB at processing site, onsells through own buyer relationships | Contractor lacks own offtake infrastructure (most Tier-2 / Tier-3 contractors); maximum value capture for VA |
| **B — Brokerage + commission** | VA introduces qualified offtake buyers to contractor, takes commission per cargo | Default position; lower capital, lower margin, lower complexity |
| **C — Silent on offtake** | VA's role limited to service-contract origination | Forfeits significant value; only acceptable if Tier-1 major has full internal offtake infrastructure |

Recommended position from engagement brief: **Structure B at minimum, Structure A if contractor profile is right.** The decision matrix:

- Tier-1 majors with their own commodity desks (SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, BORCO via Buckeye) → likely Structure C; brokerage commission alone
- Tier-1 environmental specialists (Veolia, CLH, Newpark, TWMA) → likely Structure B; offtake brokerage on commission
- Tier-2 LatAm specialists or partnership consortiums → likely Structure A; direct offtake position is the value-multiplier

Per engagement brief §6: **the offtake structure is the strongest demonstrable value to the fuel broker** — it's commodity intermediation, the broker's language, and not what most environmental services intermediaries can offer.
