## 1. The opportunity in two paragraphs

A fuel broker representing the buyer-side counterparty on an active VTC crude oil deal has surfaced a separate opportunity: brokering or originating an oilfield waste services contract for international JV operators (mixtas) operating in the western Orinoco Belt and southern Anzoátegui state of Venezuela. Profile: ~1M MT of authorized open-air pool waste overdue for treatment; ongoing spill remediation; ongoing drilling waste from accelerating E&P activity. Processing target 100K MT/month at $80-100/MT. Contractor retains recovered hydrocarbons (~20 % recovery, conservative).

Vector Antilles' proposed role is **brokerage + offtake intermediation**, not direct mixta or PDVSA engagement. Specifically: identification + qualification of contractor candidates from a bounded Tier-1 (~7 global majors) + Tier-2 (~7 LatAm specialists) universe; commercial structuring of the contractor relationship (service-contract framework + offtake structure); coordination with the fuel broker on commercial terms; coordination with you (counsel) on compliance framework. Operational execution of waste processing, capital commitment to the work, direct mixta engagement, direct PDVSA engagement — all explicitly out of scope.

## 3. Regulatory framework Cole assumes — please verify or correct

### 3.1 OFAC General License 48 (issued 2026-02-10)

Authorizes transactions ordinarily incident and necessary to provision of goods, technology, software, or services for the **exploration, development, or production of oil or gas in Venezuela**, including transactions involving PDVSA Entities.

Conditions Cole understands apply (please confirm or correct):

| Condition | Cole's understanding |
|---|---|
| Governing law | US-entity contractor's contract with Government of Venezuela / PDVSA Entity must specify US law |
| Dispute resolution | Must occur in the United States |
| Payments to blocked persons | Must route to Foreign Government Deposit Funds (FGDF) under EO 14373 of 2026-01-09, or other Treasury-instructed accounts. Excludes local taxes, permits, fees. |
| Reporting | US-entity contractor must report to State + Energy Departments within specified timelines after first transactions |

**Question:** which of these conditions apply *only* to US entities entering contracts with PDVSA Entities directly, versus which apply transitively to non-US-person intermediaries (like Vector Antilles) whose engagement is GL-48-authority-derivative?

### 3.2 FAQ 1247 — non-US person safe harbor

Cole's understanding: non-US persons engaging in Venezuela-related transactions authorized under the post-2026 GLs do not face US secondary sanctions, provided they comply with the GL conditions and were organized under the laws of a third country on or before 2025-01-29.

**Question:** assuming Vector Antilles meets the temporal threshold, what specific GL 48 compliance conditions apply to a non-US-person *consulting and offtake intermediary* whose role is contractor identification + commercial structuring + recovered-hydrocarbon offtake, but not direct contracting with PDVSA?

### 3.3 Adjacent GLs Cole expects to bear on the engagement

Please confirm relevance + flag any conditions Cole hasn't anticipated:

- **GL 46A** — purchase / export / sale / refining of already-extracted Venezuelan-origin oil by established US entities. *Relevant if recovered hydrocarbons are sold to US-entity buyers.*
- **GL 47** (2026-02-03) — transactions for export of US-origin diluents to Venezuela, including transactions involving PDVSA. *Relevant if the contractor needs diluents in the recovery / processing chain.*
- **GL 50A** — dispute resolution + contract enforcement transactions.
- **GL 51A / GL 52 / GL 54** — Venezuelan-origin minerals trading (now broader than gold). *Relevant if recovery includes minerals or rare earths beyond hydrocarbons (the broker referenced 20 % crude + minerals + rare earths recovery).*

## 6. Proposed compensation structure (subject to your review)

- **Origination commission** on signed service contract — typical industry range 1.5–3 % of total contract value, OR fixed fee per unit processed
- **Offtake position** on recovered hydrocarbons — three structural options depending on contractor profile:
  - *Direct offtake*: Vector Antilles purchases recovered crude FOB at processing site, onsells through own buyer relationships (maximum value capture; for Tier-2 LatAm contractors without commodity desks)
  - *Brokerage commission*: Vector Antilles introduces qualified offtake buyers to the contractor, takes commission per cargo (default position for Tier-1 environmental specialists)
  - *No offtake role*: only when Tier-1 major has its own integrated commodity desk
- **Optional success fee** tied to specific milestones (first cargo lifted, first quarter at run-rate)

**Question:** any structural concerns with offtake routing through Vector Antilles given the EO 14373 / FGDF framework? Recovered hydrocarbons are technically Venezuelan-origin oil whose ownership transfers from PDVSA / mixta to the contractor as part of the service contract; we assume FGDF doesn't bind a contractor's onsale of those barrels but want explicit confirmation.
