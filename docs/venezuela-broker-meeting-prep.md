## The single most important thing to remember

**The broker relationship is the asset. The waste opportunity is leverage.**

After this conversation, the broker should walk away thinking *"Cole is a sophisticated, reliable counterparty I want to work with on the crude deal and on future opportunities."* — regardless of whether the waste deal closes.

If only that happens, the engagement was successful. The crude deal closing is the second metric. The waste contract signing is the third.

## Recommended opening framing (memorize the shape, not the wording)

> "I've evaluated the opportunity you mentioned. The framework that makes it work is **GL 48 plus FAQ 1247 secondary-sanctions safe harbor for Vector Antilles as a non-US person**. The qualified contractor universe is bounded — roughly 7 global majors and 5-7 Latin American specialists. My approach is to do the structured intelligence on each candidate's current posture, identify the right contractor for this specific opportunity, and structure the commercial framework. The value I'd bring beyond identification is the **offtake position on the recovered hydrocarbons**, which most service contractors can't handle internally. If you want to share the opportunity with someone who can move it forward systematically rather than over-promise, I'd like to be that person."

This framing demonstrates four things in sequence:
1. Regulatory sophistication (specific GL framework)
2. Operational understanding (bounded universe, not wishful thinking)
3. Value-add beyond brokerage (offtake position — your language)
4. Disciplined non-overpromising

## Lead with the offtake position

This is the strongest demonstrable differentiator. Most environmental-services intermediaries can identify contractors. **Almost none can offtake the recovered hydrocarbons.**

At 20% recovery on 1M MT legacy waste alone:
- ~200K MT of recovered crude over the legacy treatment phase
- + ongoing recovery from new spill / drilling waste streams
- → meaningful commodity flow needing a buyer

| Structure | When |
|---|---|
| **A — Vector Antilles takes offtake direct** (FOB processing site, onsell through your network) | Tier-2 LatAm contractor or partnership consortium with no commodity desk — *maximum value capture* |
| **B — Vector Antilles brokers offtake on commission** | Tier-1 environmental specialists (Veolia, CLH) without integrated commodity arms — *default position* |
| **C — Silent on offtake** | Tier-1 majors with their own commodity desks (SLB, Halliburton); brokerage fee on service contract only |

Anchor on Position B at minimum. Mention Position A is available if the contractor lacks commodity infrastructure.

## What to avoid saying

- **Don't claim relationships you don't have yet.** "I have introductions to SLB" before doing the outreach is overpromising. "I have a structured approach to the SLB introduction" is honest and equally credible.
- **Don't speculate on mixta names.** Let the broker provide them.
- **Don't downplay the sanctions complexity.** The complexity is exactly the value-add — if it were simple, the broker wouldn't need you.
- **Don't price the engagement before counsel clears the structure.** "Origination commission in the 1.5–3 % industry range plus offtake position" — leave the specifics for after structure clarity.

## Questions to ask the broker (when conversation is going well)

Sequence: only after counsel has cleared information-gathering as safe to perform.

1. **Permits**: who holds the 100-hectare authorization? What's the validity window? What's the transfer mechanism if I introduce a contractor — does the contractor inherit the permit, or is a new application needed?
2. **Lab analysis**: is the waste-classification documentation available? Pool-by-pool characterization will determine treatment approach (thermal desorption vs biological vs solidification vs co-processing).
3. **Satellite imagery**: you mentioned satellite photos. Can those be shared? Pool extents help validate the 1M MT volume.
4. **Mixtas**: which JV operators specifically are seeking this contract? (Don't speculate; let them tell you.)
5. **Exclusivity**: am I the only intermediary you're discussing this with, or are others involved?
6. **Timeline**: what's the expected commercial cycle from contractor selection to operational mobilization? The Ley Penal pressure suggests fast — confirm.
7. **Recovered hydrocarbons**: have the mixtas formed a view on whether the contractor offtakes recovered crude separately from the service contract, or whether it's bundled? The offtake structure depends on this.

## Walk-away triggers — re-read these before the call

These are documented now, so they apply when invested:

- Counsel determines GL 48 framework doesn't cleanly cover the proposed engagement structure
- Discovery that the broker source or upstream parties have undisclosed sanctions exposures
- Mixta or contractor counterparties include SDN-listed individuals or entities not covered by general license
- Operational structures that require Vector Antilles to take direct PDVSA-side risk
- Contracting structure requires payment routing that doesn't comply with EO 14373 / FGDF framework
- DIB unwilling to clear the proposed payment flows
- Pressure to engage operationally before counsel clearance on specific structures
- The "permits" referenced are not what they appear to be (forged, expired, transferable but not held by broker)

The most likely failure mode in this kind of conversation is *prioritizing the waste opportunity to the detriment of the underlying broker relationship*. Discipline matters most when the walk-away cost feels highest.
